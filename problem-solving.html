Problem-Solving

Restaurant Digital Transformation

As Chief Technology Officer (CTO) for a local restaurant, I use web development skills to solve real business problems – focusing on customer engagement, modern communication, and streamlined bookings.

The Problem

Riverside Bistro relies heavily on walk-ins and phone calls. Customers search online for opening hours, menu highlights, or a way to request a table – but the information is scattered across old posts and basic directory listings.

As CTO, my challenge is to turn this fragmented experience into a clear, modern journey that works smoothly on both mobile and desktop devices.

Digital problems and web solutions

Digital Problem Web Solution
Customers struggle to find clear opening hours and contact details.
Dedicated “Visit & Contact” section with all essential details.
Booking requests arrive through texts, social messages, and calls.
Single booking and enquiry form that generates a structured email.
Menu information is inconsistent and varies across platforms.
Curated “Popular dishes” preview with clear names and prices.
Social media links are buried or not easily accessible.
Clean “Follow Riverside” section with links to Facebook, Instagram, and X.

Proposed Solution: Unified Support & Booking Hub

Build a single, mobile-friendly web page that acts as the restaurant’s digital front desk, giving customers one place to check the menu, request a booking, and choose how they want to get in touch.

Clear contact info Address, map details, and opening hours in one place.
Guided booking form Captures date, time, party size, and enquiry type.
Menu highlights Popular dishes and prices showcased for quick decisions.
Social integration Links to social channels for ongoing engagement.

Outcome & Reflection

The support hub shows how a focused front-end solution can improve the customer journey while staying realistic for a small local restaurant.

For customers

A simple, app-like experience that works well on mobile and desktop.

  • One place to see opening hours and contact details.
  • Menu highlights available before they visit.
  • Guided booking form instead of awkward phone calls.

For the restaurant

More organised enquiries and a stronger digital first impression.

  • Consistent email format makes it easier to respond quickly.
  • Booking details are captured in one message instead of multiple messages.
  • Social media presence is clearly linked and promoted.

For my CTO role

A practical example of using web skills to solve a real business need.

  • Planned and delivered a complete user journey for bookings.
  • Applied HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to generate dynamic content.
  • Designed with responsiveness and accessibility in mind.

In future, this prototype could be extended with a live booking system or online payments, but it already delivers a realistic, low-cost step in the restaurant’s digital transformation journey.
